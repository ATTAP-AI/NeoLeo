# NeoLeo Help Guide

**New Art Forms for a New Age**

---

**"NeoLeo"** takes its name from "New" and Leonardo da Vinci. Your studio for creating entirely new non-appropriative visual art forms which do not use or access any AI models or web-based libraries or AI-based corpuses of any artist's work, historical art, photos, or images. NeoLeo enables you to imagine, create, and visualize using intuitive tools that generate images based on mathematics, nature, and code — not filters on photos, but original visual art generated from visual "first principles" from scratch.

Any and all work you create using NeoLeo **belongs to you** and is not uploaded to NeoLeo or shared to any other platform. You may, of course, download any of your creations and do whatever you wish from there.

NeoLeo is entirely open source and intended (as a platform for creative artists and developers) to be used to further develop its tools and opportunities for all artists.

## Getting Started

Creating your first piece of art is simple:

- **Pick an engine** — Open the Engines section in the right panel and click any engine name. Each one produces a completely different style of visual output.
- **Press CREATE** — The large button at the top generates a new image. Every press creates something unique.
- **Change the palette** — Open the Palette section and choose a different color scheme. The same engine with a different palette gives you an entirely new mood.
- **Choose your canvas shape** — Open the Image Creation Window section to pick a shape for your canvas — square, landscape, portrait, widescreen, or Free Form to draw your own custom shape using polygon or hand-drawn modes.
- **Experiment freely** — There is no wrong way to use NeoLeo. Try different engines, adjust the sliders, layer multiple generations together.
- **Lock your seed** — The seed number controls the randomness. Click LOCK to keep the same seed while you change palettes or settings, letting you explore variations of the same composition.

## The Creation Engines (48 Styles)

Engines are the heart of NeoLeo. Each one uses a different mathematical or natural process to generate original artwork. They are grouped by character:

**Flow and Motion** — Flow Field, Strange Attractor, Boid Flocking, Curl Noise Sheets, Magnetic Field Lines, Gravity Wells, Lissajous Mesh, Oscilloscope. These create graceful, sweeping forms inspired by the physics of movement.

**Nature and Growth** — Differential Growth, Physarum (Slime Mold), Fungal Network, Cell Growth, Space Colonization, L-System Plants, Reaction-Diffusion, Watercolor, Diatom. These mimic the organic patterns found in living things.

**Geometry and Pattern** — Voronoi Diagram, Truchet Tiles, Penrose Tiling, Spirograph, Weave and Knot, Apollonian Gasket, Space-Filling Curve, ASCII Density Map, Mobius Torus, Recursive Subdivision. These explore the beauty of mathematical structure.

**Fractals and Mathematics** — Julia and Mandelbrot sets, Newton's Fractal, IFS and Barnsley, Flame Fractal, Ridged Multifractal, Domain-Warped Noise, Chladni Figures. These reveal the infinite complexity hidden in simple equations.

**Landscape and Terrain** — Contour Map, Hydraulic Erosion, Cave and Dungeon Map, Sand Drift, Marble and Wood. These generate terrain-like surfaces and natural textures.

**Systems and Simulation** — Game of Life, Langton's Ant, Waveform Collapse, Force-Directed Graph, Minimum Spanning Tree, Delaunay Triangulation, Crystal Growth. These simulate emergent behavior from simple rules.

Each engine has its own set of sliders that control its unique parameters. Move them to dramatically change the output.

## Color Palettes

Every engine renders using the currently selected color palette. Changing the palette completely transforms the look of any generation.

- Open the **Palette** section in the right panel to see the available palettes.
- Click any palette name to switch to it. The color swatches at the bottom of the image creation window update to show the active colors.
- The same engine and seed with a different palette will produce the same composition but in entirely different colors.
- You can use the **Rand** button to randomly combine an engine and palette for unexpected results.

## Drawing and Painting Tools

The toolbar on the left side of the image creation window gives you traditional drawing tools to paint directly onto the image creation window or on top of your generated art:

- **Brush** (B) — A soft, pressure-sensitive painting tool.
- **Pencil** (P) — A hard-edged drawing tool for precise lines.
- **Line** (L) — Draw straight lines between two points.
- **Rectangle** (R), **Ellipse** (E), **Triangle** (T) — Draw geometric shapes. Choose stroke only, fill only, or both.
- **Polygon** (G) — Click to place corners, then close the shape.
- **Free Shape** (S) — Draw freely and the shape closes automatically when you release.
- **Fill** (F) — Flood-fill an area with the current color.
- **Color Replace** (C) — Replace one color with another by painting over it.
- **Crop** — Trim the image creation window to a selected area.

Use the controls in the Drawing Tools section to adjust **size**, **opacity**, **hardness**, and **color**.

### Mark Color Picker

Whenever a mark-making tool is active, a small floating panel anchors next to the vertical toolbar with two swatches:

- **LAST** — Recolors the most recently placed mark in place. Pick a new color and the pixels of your last stroke change to it while preserving softness and opacity. Repeated picks keep targeting that same original mark until you place a new one.
- **NEXT** — Sets the color the next mark will be drawn with. This stays in sync with the main color picker.

The picker disappears for tools that don't make marks (like Crop), and reappears as soon as you switch back to a mark-making tool.

## Brushes (26 Types)

NeoLeo includes 26 unique brush types organized in three families. Click the brush icon in the Drawing Tools section to open the Brush Picker.

**Western Brushes** — Soft Round, Hard Round, Airbrush, Pencil, Chalk, Dry Brush, Soft Flat, Hard Flat, Spatter, and Ink Pen. These cover the full range of traditional Western painting tools.

**Chinese Calligraphy Brushes** — Seven brushes inspired by centuries of Chinese brush painting tradition, including Mao Bi (standard brush), Xie Yi (freehand), Gong Bi (fine detail), Zhu Bi (bamboo pen), Po Mo (splash ink), Ku Bi (dry brush), and Shou Jin (Emperor Huizong's slender gold style).

**Winsor and Newton Sable** — Six brushes modeled after the legendary W&N Series 7 sable brushes, including miniature, round, flat wash, rigger, and Cotman varieties.

## Advanced Image Tools

These tools let you refine and transform your artwork in powerful ways:

- **Humanize** — Adds organic imperfections to make algorithmic art feel more hand-made. Controls for wobble, pressure variation, edge roughening, ink pooling, and color drift.
- **Naturalize** — Applies film grain, color jitter, and multi-pass blending to give your art a natural, photographic quality.
- **Curves** — Fine-tune the brightness and contrast of your image using a curves editor, just like in professional photo software.
- **Gradient** — Apply smooth color gradients across the image creation window with a full gradient editor and preset library.
- **Texture** — Overlay texture patterns onto your artwork.
- **Eraser** — Remove parts of your drawing.
- **Eyedropper** — Pick a color from the image creation window to use for drawing.
- **Clone** and **Heal** — Copy areas of the image or blend them seamlessly.
- **Dodge** and **Burn** — Lighten or darken specific areas.
- **Blur**, **Sharpen**, and **Smudge** — Soften, crisp, or blend parts of your artwork.

## Experimental Art Tools

These are cutting-edge creative tools found only in NeoLeo. Open the Experimental Tools section at the bottom of the right panel:

- **Intent Sculpting** — Shape your art using invisible force fields. Adjust tension, decay, and flow to guide how forms are arranged on the image creation window.
- **Topology Engine** — Generate three-dimensional mathematical surfaces like toruses, spheres, and Klein bottles, rendered as art. Rotate and reshape them in real time.
- **Morphogenesis** — Simulate how patterns form in nature — like spots on a leopard or stripes on a zebra — using biological growth models.
- **Probability Painting** — Paint with mathematical probability distributions. Each stroke is a field of chance, creating unique textures impossible to achieve by hand.
- **Memory Drawing** — Inscribe text and ideas that become part of the visual texture of your artwork.
- **Temporal Canvases** — Create art that evolves over time, blending and shifting through different states.
- **Organic Forms** — Generate smooth, sculptural shapes from pure mathematics, rendered at full resolution with antialiasing. Five systems are included:
  - **Metaballs** — Merging blobs like oil droplets or liquid mercury via implicit surface evaluation.
  - **Superformula** — Gielis parametric curves that produce starfish, flowers, pollen grains, and sea urchin shapes.
  - **Noise Blobs** — Fractal-noise-displaced circles drawn with smooth bezier curves for amoeba and ink-drop silhouettes.
  - **Lava Lamp** — SDF smooth-union fluids that merge and divide like cell mitosis.
  - **Radiolaria** — Radially symmetric microscopic organisms inspired by Ernst Haeckel's illustrations, with concentric shells, lattice structures, and decorative spines.
  
  Use the five sliders to sculpt each form, or click Rand for instant surprises.
- **Modality Cycler** — Found within Intent Sculpting, the Cycle button steps through eight named force-field modalities (such as Tension Web, Fragile Emergence, and Pure Symmetry), each emphasizing a different aesthetic principle to discover unexpected combinations.

Use the **Connect** button to blend experimental tool output on top of your engine generation.

## The Cycle Buttons — Guided Discovery

Scattered throughout NeoLeo you will find bright red **Cycle** buttons. These are your discovery tools — each click takes you on a guided tour through an entire family of algorithms, with every setting turned up to full expression so you can see each style at its most dramatic. Think of them as a slideshow of possibilities.

**How they work:** Every Cycle button follows the same simple pattern — click it and it advances to the next option in the series, generates the result immediately, and shows you where you are (for example, "3/48 — Voronoi Diagram"). Keep clicking to explore them all, or stop when something catches your eye and start customizing from there.

Here is every Cycle button in NeoLeo:

- **Engine Cycle** — The big one. Steps through all **48 creation engines** one by one, with every parameter set to maximum. This is the fastest way to discover the full range of visual styles NeoLeo can produce — from flowing particle fields to fractal mathematics to organic growth simulations. Each click is a completely different world.
- **Intent Sculpting Modality Cycle** — Tours **8 named force-field modalities**: Tension Web (dense crossed filaments), Fragile Emergence (trembling branches), Pure Symmetry (mirrored organic forms), Dissolution Field (ghostly fading marks), Desire Lines (tight loops and spirals), Resistance Storm (turbulent scattered marks), Full Force (maximal complexity), and Calm Tension (long ordered strands). Each one gives your art a completely different emotional character.
- **Temporal Canvases Cycle** — Steps through **5 time-based modes**: Emergence (calm building into structure), Dissolution (order melting into chaos), Oscillation (wave-like memory), Remembrance (art that recalls itself), and Entropy (everything slowly forgetting). These create art that feels like it is alive and changing.
- **Probability Painting Cycle** — Moves through different **probability expressions** — each one changes how chance and mathematics shape your brush strokes, producing textures that are impossible to create by hand.
- **Memory Drawing Cycle** — Cycles through different **neural architectures** that transform your inscribed text and ideas into visual textures, each one interpreting your words in a different visual language.
- **Morphogenesis Cycle** — Tours **5 biological growth systems**: Reaction-Diffusion (Turing patterns like animal markings), Phylogenetic (evolutionary branching), Branching (tree-like growth), Chladni (vibration patterns on a surface), and Voronoi (cell-like territory maps). Nature's own design principles, one click at a time.
- **Topology Engine Cycle** — Steps through **mathematical surfaces** — toruses, Klein bottles, spheres, and other shapes from the field of topology, each one rendered as a unique piece of three-dimensional art.
- **Organic Forms Cycle** — Tours **5 sculptural form systems**: Metaballs (merging blobs), Superformula (Gielis parametric shapes), Noise Blobs (amoeba silhouettes), Lava Lamp (smooth-union fluids), and Radiolaria (Haeckel-inspired microscopic forms).

The Cycle buttons are designed for playful exploration. You do not need to understand the mathematics behind any of these — just click, look, and follow your eye to what inspires you.

## Happy Hallucinations

**Happy Hallucinations (HH)** is NeoLeo's signature creative feature. It automatically composes a complete artwork by randomly combining an engine, palette, and drawing tools into a single layered composition. Each press of the HH button creates a completely unique piece with a descriptive mood name.

**Experimental Tool Blending:** HH and Multi-Pass now have a 40% chance per pass of layering output from one of NeoLeo's six experimental tools — Organic Forms, Morphogenesis, Topology Engine, Probability Painting, Memory Drawing, and Temporal Canvases. When an experimental tool fires, its tag appears in the mood label (e.g. *organic:nblob*, *morpho:rd*, *topo:shape*). In Multi-Pass mode, at least one pass is guaranteed to include an experimental layer, producing richly complex compositions that blend algorithmic engines with sculptural forms, biological growth patterns, and mathematical surfaces.

**Multi-Pass Blend** takes this further — it runs up to **10 passes** and layers them together with adjustable opacity and decay. The result is rich, complex artwork that no single generation could produce. Use the sliders to control how the passes blend together.

## Lighting and Atmosphere

Add cinematic lighting and mood to your artwork using the toolbar buttons on the left:

**Lighting** (click the light bulb icon) — Add up to two light sources to illuminate your art. Choose from point lights, spotlights, or rim lighting. Adjust color, intensity, radius, bloom, and ambient fill to create dramatic illumination.

**Atmosphere** (click the cloud icon) — Layer atmospheric effects on top of your art:

- **Vignette** — Darken the edges to draw focus to the center.
- **Fog and Haze** — Add a soft, misty overlay.
- **Film Grain** — Simulate analog film texture.
- **Color Grading** — Shift the overall temperature, saturation, contrast, and brightness.
- **Chromatic Aberration** — Add subtle color fringing like a vintage lens.

## Layers

Layers let you stack multiple images on top of each other, just like transparent sheets of paper:

**Drawing Layers** — Add multiple drawing layers so you can paint on separate surfaces. Each layer can have its own blend mode (how it mixes with layers below) and opacity (how see-through it is). Reorder, duplicate, merge, or flatten layers as needed.

**Engine Layers** — Capture different engine generations as separate layers and composite them together. Each engine layer has its own blend mode and opacity slider.

**Auto-Layer** — Toggle the Auto button in the Engine Layers section. When on, every new generation automatically becomes its own layer instead of replacing the previous one. This lets you rapidly build up complex compositions from multiple engines.

**Blend Modes** — These control how layers combine: Normal stacks them, Multiply darkens, Screen brightens, Overlay adds contrast, and many more. Experiment to find combinations you like.

## Free Form Canvas

Create a custom-shaped Image Creation Window instead of the standard rectangular canvas:

- Open the Image Creation Window section and select the **Free Form** swatch from the shape options.
- A mode selection menu appears with two drawing modes:

**Polygon Mode** — Click anywhere on the canvas to place vertices one at a time. Each click adds a corner point connected by straight lines. You need at least three points to form a shape. Double-click, press Enter, or click near the first point to close the shape. Press Backspace to remove the last point, or Escape to cancel.

**Hand Drawn Mode** — Click and hold the mouse button, then drag freely to draw any shape you like. The line follows your mouse movement naturally. When you release the mouse, NeoLeo automatically smooths your drawing into a clean, antialiased curve using corner-cutting refinement, so even a rough sketch becomes an elegant shape.

Once your shape is complete, the Image Creation Window is clipped to your custom outline. You can then generate art, paint, and use all tools within your custom shape. The background color you have selected fills the area outside your shape.

Use **Undo** and **Redo** to step back and forward through freeform changes, and **Reset** to return to a standard square canvas.

## Uploading and Compositing Images

You can bring your own images into NeoLeo to combine with generated art:

- **Upload** — Click the upload button (top of the toolbar) or drag and drop a PNG or JPEG image onto the upload area. Your image appears on the image creation window.
- **Layer Mode** — Choose whether your image sits *under* the generated art, *over* it, or *replaces* it entirely.
- **Opacity and Blend** — Control how transparent your image is and how it blends with the generated art.
- **Image Signal** — Extract the structure and colors from your uploaded image and use them to guide how engines generate. The engine will follow the shapes and palette of your photo.
- **Bake to Image** — Permanently merge your uploaded image into the generated image.
- **Image Adjustments** — Fine-tune brightness, contrast, saturation, exposure, highlights, shadows, temperature, and tint of your uploaded image.

## AI Creative Assistant

NeoLeo includes an optional AI assistant that can help you explore creative directions. Click the AI button in the toolbar (or press Shift+P) to open the prompt panel.

Describe what you want in plain language — for example, "make it warmer and more dramatic" or "switch to something organic and flowing" — and the assistant will adjust the engine, palette, lighting, and atmosphere settings to match your vision.

The AI only changes settings and parameters. It does not generate images, access external data, or use any pre-existing artwork. All visual output is still created entirely by NeoLeo's own engines.

## Saving and Exporting Your Art

- **PNG Export** — Click the PNG button (top bar) to export your artwork as a high-quality PNG image file that downloads to your computer.
- **Save State** — Click Save to store your current settings (engine, palette, seed, sliders) so you can return to them later. You can save up to 8 named states.
- **Generation History** — NeoLeo keeps a visual history of your recent generations. Click any thumbnail in the history strip to restore that generation.
- **Undo** and **Redo** — Use the Undo and Redo buttons (top left) or keyboard shortcuts to step backward and forward through your changes.

## Showcase Gallery

The **Showcase** is a curated gallery of **87 pre-configured artworks** spanning every engine, tool, and style in NeoLeo. It serves as both inspiration and a learning tool — each thumbnail shows you what a particular engine or tool combination can produce.

- **8 Categories** — Artworks are organized by type: Flow & Motion, Nature & Growth, Geometry & Pattern, Fractals & Math, Landscape & Terrain, Systems & Simulation, Experimental, and Happy Hallucinations. Click a category tab to filter the gallery.
- **Click any thumbnail** to instantly load that artwork's exact engine, palette, seed, and settings into the Image Creation Window. NeoLeo will generate a fresh render using those parameters.
- **Menu Highlighting** — When you click a showcase thumbnail, the corresponding engine or tool in the main menu is automatically highlighted with a blinking yellow outline so you can see exactly which feature created that artwork.
- **Replace vs. Additive Mode** — Toggle between replacing the current canvas entirely or layering the showcase artwork on top of your existing work.
- **My Gallery** — Save your own favorite creations to a personal gallery within Showcase for quick recall.

An **"Image Rendering..."** notification appears in the center of the screen while the showcase artwork is being generated, so you know the system is working.

## Background Color and Auto Re-Render

The background color sets the base color behind your artwork. You can change it using the preset dots or the square color picker in the Background section of the right panel.

- **Auto Re-Render** — When you change the background color while an engine image is displayed, NeoLeo automatically re-renders the current artwork onto the new background. This happens with a short debounce delay (180ms) so you can drag through colors smoothly without triggering excessive renders.
- **Lighting & Atmosphere preserved** — After the re-render, any active lighting and atmosphere effects are automatically re-applied on top of the new background.
- The re-render uses the same seed and settings, so you see the exact same composition — just on a different background color.
